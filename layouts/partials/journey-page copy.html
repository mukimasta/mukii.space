Journey
Not a resume. Just the moments that mattered.
CHILDHOOD
Taking things apart
Every device that entered the house eventually got disassembled. Not to break it — to see how it worked. Most of them went back together. Some didn't.
2016
First contact
Sophomore year, electrical engineering major. Stumbled into computer systems — how a CPU actually executes instructions, how memory works, how everything connects. Couldn't sleep for days. The university didn't offer this course for my major. So I found MIT's lectures online, worked through them alone, and designed a CPU that ran on an FPGA. No grade. No assignment. Just needed to touch it.
FPGA board photo
2019
National scholarship
Xidian University. One of the few awards I actually cared about — not for the credential, but because it confirmed I wasn't delusional for caring this much.
2022
Tsinghua AIR Lab
Research on open-vocabulary robotic grasping. First time working at the intersection of language and physical manipulation. Learned what research rigor actually means.
2023
The robot moved
MagicLab Robotics. Fine-tuned a 3.3B parameter VLA model. At the WAIC exhibition, the Franka arm followed spoken commands and picked up objects in real time. A crowd gathered around the demo. Months of debugging JAX code, and then — it just worked.
WAIC demo photo
2024
Crossed the Pacific
Started the ECE Master's at University of Washington. New city, new system, new problems to solve.
2024
5 million states, one bug
Course project: build a distributed KV store with Multi-Paxos. Most people stopped when it passed the tests. I wrote a BFS model checker, explored 5 million+ system states, and found a real consensus bug in the P2A retry logic. Fixed it. Then it was actually correct.
SOMETIME
A melody that wouldn't leave
Made electronic music because something was stuck in my head and wouldn't go away until I got it out.
